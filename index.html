Guided Walkthrough

Click one of these and the calculator will switch tabs and highlight the right section.

Overview

This calculator is for category-weighted classes. Categories like Assignments, Quizzes, and Tests affect your class grade by different weights. Grades inside each category are based on total points earned divided by total points possible.

Class Setup

Give the class a name so you can save it. You can also set a current goal grade. Saved classes can be loaded later and used by the all-classes planner.

What to do:
  1. Type the class name.
  2. Optionally type your goal grade.
  3. Click Save Class.

Importing a Gradebook

Paste your gradebook exactly how it copies from your school site. The importer tries to read category names, category weights, assignment names, valid grades, and missing grades. Missing grades should count as zeroes.

Fastest method:
  1. Copy your whole gradebook.
  2. Paste it into the importer box.
  3. Click Import Pasted Gradebook.

Categories and Grades

Add one category for each type of work, such as Assignments, Quizzes, or Tests. Put in the category weight. Then add grades underneath that category. You can give each assignment a name and enter points earned and points possible.

Example:
  • Assignments = 35
  • Quizzes = 25
  • Tests = 40

Corrections

If your class allows corrections, you can set a correction cap for a category. Then eligible grades in that category can be corrected up to that cap. Grades below 70 are not correction-eligible.

Insights

The insights tab shows what is hurting your grade the most, your best missing assignment to turn in, your best correction option, your weakest category, whether a strong letter grade still looks reachable, and risk warnings based on your saved upcoming work.

Solver

The solver lets you add upcoming assignments and see what scores you need to hit a target grade. You can also find the lowest score you can get on one future assignment while staying above a chosen class grade.

All Classes Planner

The all-classes planner compares all your saved classes and shows the moves that could help the most, like turning in a missing assignment or correcting a quiz. If nothing shows up, make sure your classes are saved and your correction caps are set in the Grades tab.

Paste Gradebook Import

Paste your gradebook exactly how it copies from your school page. Missing grades count as zeroes. Importing replaces your current categories and grades.

Add Category

Make one category for each type of grade. Set the weight, and if needed, a correction cap percent for that category. Grades below 70 are not correctable.

Categories

This shows all your categories and assignments. You can add assignment names, correct eligible grades, edit or delete grades, and change category rules.

Current Grade Summary

This shows your current grade, your letter grade, and how each category is affecting your grade.

Insights & Warnings

This section shows what is hurting your grade the most, whether common grade targets are still reachable, and warnings about risky scores.

Target / Future Grade Solver

Use this to see what grades you need in the future. Add upcoming grades, then click Solve.

Upcoming Grades

Add a slot for each future grade. Choose its category, points possible, and optional name. You can also check Drop lowest in category if the new grade would replace the lowest grade there.

Lowest Safe Score

Use this to find the lowest score you can get on one future grade without your class grade dropping below a target.

Solver Results

This shows your results. It includes best case, worst case, what you need on each grade, and example score combinations.
